# MINERD — Matemática Nivel Secundario

> **Source:** Adecuación Curricular Nivel Secundario — Área de Matemática (MINERD 2023)
> **Relevance:** Secondary curriculum reference for EducaT Phase 2 (Grades 7–12 expansion)

## Definición del Área

Math curriculum grounded in constant transformation and development — values both processes and results. Expressed as three curricular perspectives: **herramienta para la vida · herramienta intelectual · herramienta de conexión y desarrollo de otras ciencias**.

Higher-level ambition: autonomous and rigorous reasoning, clarity/precision in communication, integration of information technologies, collaborative work, creative/flexible response to changing situations.

---

## Propósito del Área

Integral development of Fundamental Competencies from formative characteristics of the area. Through Specific Area Competencies, contribute to: student exit profile as citizen capable of exercising rights/duties, joining active life, and continuing formation at technical or university level. Develop complex mathematical thinking for autonomous and creative knowledge construction throughout life.

## Metodología — Primer Ciclo Secundaria

Problem solving central — not a curricular topic but a process impregnating the entire cycle. Provides context for learning concepts, developing capacities/skills, working other competencies.

**Students will learn:**
- Read and interpret environment information using mathematical language
- Reason and argue about mental representations of environment data as logical thinking
- Develop and apply different strategies to solve mathematical problems from context situations
- Show responsibility expressing mathematical ideas in environment/community situations
- Identify and use applications and technological tools in mathematics and interpret environment situations
- Respect others' ideas in mathematical group work
- Make and evaluate mathematical arguments and conjectures, validating own ideas
- Make mathematical connections to other disciplines and knowledge areas, identifying diverse factors of context, health, environment
- Develop conceptual structures on mathematical topics, including role of definitions

**Evaluation types:** Diagnostic · Formative · Summative
**Evaluation actors:** Autoevaluación · Coevaluación · Heteroevaluación

## Mallas Curriculares — Primer Ciclo Secundaria

### 1er. Grado Secundaria

#### Contenidos Conceptuales

**Numeración:**
- Integers and their operations
- Rational numbers and their operations
- Simple and compound interest, capital and amount, monthly/annual rate
- Taxes: origin and associated problems
- Economy and its main areas of study

**Geometría:**
- Lines and their types, angles and their types
- Pythagorean theorem
- Right prisms and their characteristics
- Cube, lateral and total surface area of regular right prisms
- Volume, edge, vertex

**Medición:**
- Units of measure, volume of prisms and pyramids
- Cubic units (arbitrary and metric): cubic meter, cubic decimeter, cubic centimeter, cubic millimeter
- Equivalence of cubic units using multiples/submultiples
- Volume of rectangular right prisms
- Volume of solids formed by several right prisms
- Operations with cubic units of metric system
- Capacity as internal volume of containers
- Metric capacity units: liter, milliliter, equivalence dm³ = liter

**Estadística:**
- Collection, organization, grouping of grouped data
- Statistical graphs for grouped data (histogram, frequency polygon)
- Measures of central tendency
- Simple random experiment events
- Probability of occurrence for simple events

#### Contenidos Procedimentales

- Identification, reading, writing of integers
- Reading, writing, representation of rational numbers
- Determination of absolute value of rational number
- Problem solving with rational numbers in daily life
- Analysis of etymology and definition of economy concept
- Classification of economy's main areas of study
- Classification/analysis of resources as elements in economic actions
- Projects: analysis of economic/entrepreneurial actions for sustainable environment
- Problem solving with percent, simple interest, capital, and amount
- Construction of parallel/perpendicular lines, bisectors of different angles
- Calculation of angle complements and supplements
- Problem solving applying Pythagorean theorem
- Projects with volume as capacity unit with cubic units of metric system
- Volume calculation of rectangular right prisms; operations with metric cubic units
- Capacity estimation in containers from simulations/models with metric decimal units
- Use of Cartesian coordinate system for point location in plane and figure tracing
- Conversions: tons to grams and kilograms
- Elaboration of schedules and chronograms, time comparison in different units
- Temperature identification in thermometers
- Construction and interpretation of grouped data statistical graphs (histogram, frequency polygon)
- Determination and application of central tendency measures
- Construction of sample spaces for simple events
- Calculation of probabilities for simple events

#### Contenidos Actitudinales (1ro Secundaria)

- Interest in clearly and coherently communicating mathematical ideas involving integers and rationals
- Enjoyment in solving/analyzing economic and financial actions
- Interest in creating/using graphic representations of Pythagorean theorem and Cartesian axis figures using software
- Appreciation of importance of measurement units for daily problem solving
- Rigor, flexibility, originality in area and volume problem solving
- Interest in creating/using graphic representations of statistical data through electronic devices
- Rigor, flexibility, originality in processes of calculating central tendency statistical measures

**Indicadores de Logro (1ro Secundaria):**
- Uses mathematical language (oral, written, graphic) describing environment situations using integers, rationals, and their operations
- Formulates and solves variety of problems with integers and rationals, from situations inside/outside mathematics
- Expresses in own words (oral/written) ideas on economic/financial concepts and application areas
- Recognizes/applies deductive/inductive reasoning in problem situations associated with math and finances (simple/compound interest, capital, amount, monthly/annual rate, taxes)
- Explains with convincing, analysis-based arguments about central tendency measures, current/community topics
- Explains and constructs grouped data statistical graphs (histograms, frequency polygons) in analysis/solution of real problems
- Expresses through inductive reasoning results associated with probability calculation for simple random experiment events
- Evaluates arguments based on data/statistical graph analysis
- Reads/interprets graphic representations of points, lines, geometric figures in Cartesian coordinate system
- Represents/solves problems through geometric models including Pythagorean theorem applications
- Identifies, describes, compares, classifies different geometric objects in line/angle problem situations
- Supports with concrete/digital learning resources: concept construction, area/volume formula development, geometric proposition experimentation/demonstration
- Visualizes, represents, classifies geometric solids (prisms and pyramids)
- Formulates/analyzes conjectures on 3D figures (prisms/pyramids), volume relationships, and surface area relationships
- Solves context problems inside/outside math, correctly uses math notation for length, volume, weight, capacity units of metric system
- Understands/applies equivalences/relationships between length, capacity, weight/mass measures in context problem solving
- Exposes mathematical reasoning as support in time/temperature problem solving
- Visualizes/constructs (with appropriate instruments and tech applications) parallel/perpendicular lines, angle bisectors, geometric figures in general
- Integrates technologies (math applications, simulations, internet, calculators) to support understanding of mathematical ideas and problem solving
- Explains, with graphs and mathematical models, main problems related to diseases affecting people's health
- Works collaboratively respecting different viewpoints in mathematical work interpretation

## SEGUNDO CICLO — Síntesis de Competencias y Contenidos

> For reference in EducaT Phase 2 planning (Grades 10–12 scope).

### Contextualización

Second Cycle develops: geometric thinking, trigonometric thinking, calculus, financial mathematics, statistics. Students: solve problems, communicate mathematically, reason and argue with logical validity, integrate technologies, work collaboratively, exercise responsible citizenship.

**Three major themes:** Geometría · Trigonometría · Cálculo

### 4to. Grado Secundaria

**Focus:** Geometry deep dive

**Key Concepts:**
- Angles, measurement systems, postulates, congruence, parallelism, perpendicularity
- Polygons: diagonals, internal/external angles, perimeter
- Triangles: properties, congruence, angle relationships, sides
- Postulates: SSS (LLL), SAS (LAL), ASA (ALA)
- Circles: properties and elements, types, volume of polyhedra and truncated solids
- Geometric transformations

**Procedures:** Construction/measurement of angles with protractors, squares, compasses (physical and virtual/Proyecto Gauss) · Construction/interpretation of perpendiculars and transversals · Application of line postulates · Polygon calculations · Triangle theorem applications (interior/exterior angles) · Thales, Menelaus, Ceva theorems · Circle problem solving · Geometric transformation identification/classification · Volume calculation of polyhedra and truncated solids

**Key Logro:** Apply Polya's problem-solving method to daily life situations from angles between parallels; employ strategies for calculating number of polygon diagonals; solve volume calculation problems; resolve congruence postulate problems.

---

### 5to. Grado Secundaria

**Focus:** Conics + Vectors/Matrices + Trigonometry

**Key Concepts:**
- Conics equations: line, circle, parabola, ellipse, hyperbola and their elements
- Vectors and matrices: types, operations, determinants, inverse matrix
- Trigonometry: origin, development, trigonometric functions
- Law of sines and cosines
- Right and oblique triangle resolution
- Pythagorean trigonometric identities

**Procedures:** Analysis/interpretation of line equation · Context problem solving with conics · Vector classification/notation/representation · Matrix type identification · Vector/matrix operations · Law of sines/cosines application to daily life · Right triangle resolution · Oblique triangle calculation/resolution · Demonstration of trig functions of notable/special angles · Graphic representation of sinusoids, cosinusoids, tangentoids · Pythagorean identity verification

---

### 6to. Grado Secundaria

**Focus:** Equation theory + Sequences/Finance + Complex Numbers + Trigonometry advanced + Limits/Derivatives/Integrals

**Key Concepts:**
- Equation theory: fundamental algebra theorem
- Exponential and logarithmic equations
- Quadratic inequalities, quadratic equation systems in two variables
- Sequences, progressions, finances: arithmetic/geometric sequences, progressions
- Present value at simple/compound interest, credit card (absolute vs. unpaid balance)
- Complex numbers: ordered pair, binomial, polar, trigonometric forms
- Trigonometry: sum/difference angle formulas, double/triple/half angle, trigonometric equations
- Limits, derivatives, simple integrals
- Concept of function limits and sequences · Limit properties · Function/sequence derivatives · Derivative rules · Definite/indefinite integrals

**Procedures:** Root analysis from fundamental algebra theorem · Quadratic inequality resolution (analytical/graphic) · Quadratic equation/inequality system resolution · Exponential/logarithmic equation use in problem solving · Problem solving with bank/other loans using arithmetic/geometric sequences · Compound interest/present value calculation · Credit card analysis (interest, discounts, payment terms) · Complex number identification (binomial, polar, trigonometric) and graphic representation · Sum/difference angle formula demonstration · Trigonometric identity use · Trigonometric equation solving · Limit calculation · Derivative rules identification/analysis · Derivative calculation using limit concept · Function derivatives (algebraic, trigonometric, transcendent) using derivation rules · Definite/indefinite integral identification/analysis · Integral calculation using integration rules
